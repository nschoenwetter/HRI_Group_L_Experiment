Debriefing

Thank you for your participation in our study! Your anonymous data makes an important contribution to our understanding of robot ethics.

In this research we were particularly interested in how people perceive robots that respond to uncertain or ambiguous commands to perform impermissible actions. This is important because some robots ask for clarification whenever they are unsure how to interpret an utterance, and by doing so, may incorrectly communicate their own ethical programming or the true moral and social norms of their environments.

If you have any questions about this research, you may contact the principal investigator, Tom Williams, at twilliams@mines.edu.
